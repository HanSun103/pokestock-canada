# PokéStock Canada demo script

Target length: 2 minutes 30 seconds. Each scene is designed for approximately 15 seconds.

## 1. The collector problem — 0:00–0:15

For Canadian Pokémon TCG collectors, popular products can disappear soon after a listing appears. A normal stock alert may arrive too late. PokéStock Canada looks for earlier signals, while fair-price inventory is still worth preparing for.

## 2. The objective — 0:15–0:30

The goal is simple: help collectors prepare before fair-price products sell out. PokéStock separates three dates that are often confused: the official release, the first Canadian buying opportunity, and the latest availability check.

## 3. From public signal to collector action — 0:30–0:45

Official news and permitted feeds enter one evidence pipeline. PokéStock connects related product names, validates the dates and region, and moves each product through clear watch states. The dashboard and alerts share that same evidence.

## 4. Release radar — 0:45–1:00

The Release radar begins with official product confirmation and focuses on what collectors can do next. Products can be filtered by watch stage and sorted by the closest estimated action date, so the most useful opportunities appear first.

## 5. GPT-5.6 reasoning — 1:00–1:15

Connectors discover the source links; GPT does not browse for them. GPT-5.6 compares the supplied, dated evidence and estimates a Canadian buying window. The interface clearly separates that estimate from an official date.

## 6. Deterministic safeguards — 1:15–1:30

Deterministic rules remain authoritative. A global availability post cannot trigger a Canadian Live now alert, and weaker evidence cannot overwrite a Canadian observation. Restock watch appears only when a dated source explicitly announces a restock.

## 7. Release radar — 1:30–1:45

Release radar starts when official news confirms a product. Across seven recent releases, Pokémon Center availability followed the reveal by a median of thirteen days, with a range from the same day to forty-one days. That is the window collectors should monitor.

## 8. Learning from Pitch Black — 1:45–2:00

Pitch Black shows why freshness matters. Its July seventeenth launch notice confirmed limited Canadian inventory, but not that stock remained live the next day. Once sold out was observed, it moved from the active radar into the Canadian availability record.

## 9. Responsible by design — 2:00–2:15

PokéStock uses public sources and never automates purchases or bypasses queues. The hosted GitHub workflow receives no OpenAI API key, so scheduled updates cannot consume model credits. GPT analysis remains local and optional.

## 10. Closing — 2:15–2:30

PokéStock Canada turns scattered release signals into an earlier, calmer decision point: prepare, watch, and verify before buying. It gives Canadian collectors a fairer chance to find products at retail price.
